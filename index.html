The 10 questions worth asking

We'd rather you ask these than assume the answers. Every one is answered straight, including the ones where the honest answer is "not yet."

01What is Xtr8 actually doing under the hood?+
A deterministic technical-confluence engine, not an AI black box. It computes trend (EMA20/EMA50 alignment), momentum (RSI, MACD histogram), volatility (Bollinger Bands, ATR), and volume/participation where real volume exists — then combines them into a weighted 0–100 score. Every number it shows you, you could recalculate by hand from the same price data. Nothing is hidden from you — the Quant view shows the raw indicator values behind every call.
02Does it use order flow, footprint charts, or Level 2 data?+
Not yet, and we're not going to pretend otherwise. Real order-flow analysis (delta, footprint charts, depth-of-book) needs paid tick-level or Level 2 exchange feeds — that's genuinely expensive infrastructure, not a checkbox. It's on the roadmap for the Desk/Institutional tier once a real data feed is wired in. Today's edge is rigorous, transparent price-action confluence — a different thing, done honestly, not order flow dressed up as a buzzword.
03What timeframes does it actually read?+
Right now: a single timeframe per asset (roughly daily-to-4H resolution depending on the market). It does not yet combine 1H/4H/1D into one read — that multi-timeframe confluence is the first thing being built for v1.0, because reading one timeframe in isolation is a real limitation, not a feature.
04How exactly are entry, stop-loss and take-profit calculated?+
Plain formula, no mystery: stop-loss sits 1.5× ATR(14) from entry in the direction against the trade; take-profit is set at 2× that risk distance, for a fixed 1:2 reward-to-risk. It's disclosed so you can override it with your own risk parameters — it's a starting point, not a command.
05What's the win rate?+
We don't publish a number, because we haven't earned the right to yet. Every signal you view gets timestamped and logged. That log — not a marketing claim — becomes the actual track record over time. If a tool shows you a win rate on day one, ask where the trades came from.
06How is this different from me just reading the same indicators on TradingView myself?+
Honestly — the underlying math is the same math. The value isn't a secret indicator; it's doing that calculation consistently, instantly, across multiple assets, with the weighting and reasoning shown in one place instead of you eyeballing five charts and doing it slightly differently each time. Speed and discipline, not sorcery.
07Does it factor in news or fundamentals?+
Currently: community chatter (public forum posts) as a rough, unverified sentiment gauge — explicitly labeled as such, not presented as vetted news. Real financial news with sentiment scoring is a v1.0 feature, once it's wired through a proper news API rather than scraped forum posts.
08Can I adjust the model to match my own trading style?+
Not in this version. A custom strategy builder — where you set how much weight trend vs. momentum vs. volume carries, and save your own configuration — is planned for the final version, aimed squarely at traders and quants who want the engine, not just its default output.
09Why is there no volume reading on forex pairs?+
Because spot FX is an over-the-counter market — there's no single, centralized volume figure the way there is for a listed stock or a crypto exchange. Rather than invent a number, the model substitutes volatility expansion (change in ATR) as a disclosed proxy for participation. It's flagged as a proxy every time, not presented as real volume.
10Is this financial advice, or a guarantee of anything?+
No, and no. It's a decision-support tool built from disclosed technical-analysis math. No system — this one included — can guarantee an outcome in these markets. Position sizing, final judgment, and risk are always yours.

Why we're telling you what this can't do yet. Every trading tool that launches claiming "accurate signals" is either overselling or about to cost someone money. Xtr8's bet is the opposite: show the reasoning, log the track record in public, and let the numbers earn trust over time instead of asking for it up front.

Nothing on this page is financial advice, and no version of Xtr8 — free, Pro, or Desk — will ever guarantee an outcome. What gets better with each version is transparency, coverage, and how much of your own judgment you can plug into the model.
